Listen, I know travelling is such a cliche thing to write an "about me" style webpage about... but traveling's just really cool man. I've gotten to do it a lot in my life, and I'm really really grateful I've gotten to go to all of these places. This project was a lovely stroll down memory lane.

North America

America (duh)

I mean, what is there to say? Honestly probably anything I want since no one will actually read the words on this webpage. But the US is great (in terms of beauty and things to do, it does a lot of things wrong in other ways). I have a goal of visiting every National Park in my lifetime, which is hopefully doable, but I'm going to be like 94 by then.

Europe

Spain

Now let's get into the fun stuff! This was my study abroad to Marid, Spain. And I know it's super cliche, but this was one the best times of my life. I learned what it was like to be an adult during these 4 months. I miss the tapas.

Most Memorable Moment: Pulling an all nighter to go to Valencia and see hundreds of beautiful, huge 30ft tall paper mache sculptures burn to the ground at Las Fallas. Including one of Trump, weirdly enough.

Paris, France

I was not expected to like Paris, but I loved Paris! If I ever went back to Europe, this would be the place I revisit. I did not get to see enough. I went to Disneyland Paris... it was Disneyland. Also, why Mona Lisa so smol?

Most Memorable Moment: Having a mental breakdown in a Starbucks because I lost my Mickey Bucket Hat in a storm (pictured above).

Ireland

I was living my Ed Sheeran fantasy during this trip. I was his Gallway Girl. Turns out his Gallway Girl is cold and wet.

Most Memorable Moment: Seeing a stranger almost fall into a pit of mud, and as I try to save them without them asking for it, I--naturally, fall into the pit of despair. They were fine.

Denmark

Have you ever been to a place that has some of the highest globally ranked education, health care, public transport, and equality scores in the world? If not you should try it, there was something in the air man. You can tell the people here are just naturally happy. Everyone was so calm and blonde.

Most Memorable Moment: Nothing in particular. I just remember the vibe that everyone seemed happy. Which I know I just said, but it was a surreal thing to experience.

Most Memorable Moment: Ironically, I don't remember what happened. But I do remember on one of the days I wrote down in the notes app of my phone that it was the 3rd worst day of my life. Specifically the 3rd. No idea what happened, but it must've been bad. But not so bad to make 1st or 2nd place.

Italy

This trip was a gauntlet. We took a week and hit as many cities as we could. I'm talking Venice, Florence, Rome, Orto, and Naples in 7 days. I learned you can buy an entire pizza for €2, Venice is only like 2 sq miles big, and there's a difference between real gelato and tourist gelato. Also, got to try squid ink pasta for the first time, which was not bad.

Also this fact is technically NSFW, but it's historical and really funny, so you're going to get it. In Pompeii, brothel's were all the rage, right? They were so popular, in fact, that the city carved LITERAL DICKS into the streets and buildings that pointed in the direction of the brothel. So you would just follow the dickrection for the best 2-3 minutes of your day. For proof, please see the photo of me attempting to lick this piece of timeless history.

Most Memorable Moment: Watching a street performer spin tassels of fire clipped to his nipples.

Prague, Czech Republic

This was one of my favorite city for vibes. It was very warm toned, which I loved. Weird fact: their airport had 3 languages: Czech, English, and Korean. Go figure. We didn't do a whole lot of activities, but it was very pretty.

Most Memorable Moment: The John Lenon Wall (pictured above).

Asia

Seoul, South Korea

Probably my favorite city I've ever been to. I love it there. I want to live there. I love living in big cities, and Seoul just has so many fun and exciting things to see and do. You can never get bored there. It also introduced me to my favorite dish jjimdak (찜닭), a briased chicken stew.

Fun story: so before I left for SK, I didn't have a brick and mortar bank. So I asked my mom if she could transfer some money into Wons (₩) for me at hers, but she, understandably, heard "ones." So she comes back with like $250 all in 1 dollar bills and I got all upset like "mom, do you think South Korea is a strip club??" and my mom was like, "listen, it didn't make sense to me either, but I wasn't going to question it."

Most Memorable Moment: Promising a random Korean couple I met on a ferry that I would date their son in exchange for a free ride to the bus station, and then taking a night bus across the country to make it to my mid-term on time after I missed my flight from Jeju Island. I got a B+ and did not date their son.
